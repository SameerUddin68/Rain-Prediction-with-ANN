About RainCast AI

RainCast AI is a web application powered by an Artificial Neural Network designed to predict whether it will rain on the following day. This predictive model is based on the Rain Prediction in Australia dataset. Over 80% of Australia receives less than 600 mm of annual rainfall, ranking it among the continents with the lowest precipitation, excluding Antarctica. Inland areas, such as those near Lake Eyre, may receive as little as 81 mm of rainfall annually. The Australian desert typically experiences a low average annual rainfall ranging from 81 to 250 mm, with around 15 to 20 thunderstorms per year.

Southern Australia is characterized by westerly winds and rain-bearing cold fronts during winter, driven by high-pressure systems moving northward. Cold snaps may lead to frosts inland, while coastal temperatures remain mild throughout the year. Summers in southern Australia are generally dry and hot, with coastal sea breezes. Extended dry periods can result in bushfires, particularly in Victoria and New South Wales.

Conversely, tropical regions in northern Australia experience a wet summer due to the monsoon season, occurring typically from October to April. Humid north-westerly winds during "the wet" bring showers and thunderstorms. Tropical cyclones may also contribute to heavy rainfall along coastal areas, extending further inland.
